# About This Portfolio

This repository represents a **system-level architecture portfolio** focused on long-living Web3 protocols, data infrastructures, and incentive systems.

It is not a collection of implementations or client deliverables.  
It is a structured representation of **how architectural responsibility is defined, bounded, and applied** across complex Web3 systems.

---

## Role Definition

I operate in the role of a **Senior / Principal Web3 Systems Architect**.

My responsibility is not execution or delivery velocity, but:
- system structure
- responsibility demarcation
- invariants and guard rails
- long-term stability under change

I work at the level where:
- protocol design meets infrastructure constraints
- data systems become trusted system inputs
- governance and incentives are treated as control surfaces

---

## Scope of Architectural Ownership

Within an engagement, architectural ownership typically includes:

- defining system boundaries and interfaces
- designing modular protocol structures
- specifying invariants and safety constraints
- modeling failure modes and degradation paths
- ensuring evolvability across time horizons

Architecture does **not** absorb:
- daily execution
- operational staffing
- product prioritization
- ad-hoc decision-making

Clear scope definition is a prerequisite for system stability.

---

## Clean-room Approach

All materials in this portfolio follow a **clean-room methodology**:

- no proprietary code
- no client-specific implementations
- no reuse of internal IP
- no market or strategic interpretation

Only architecture-level structures, patterns, and system behavior models are presented.
